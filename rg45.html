RJ45 Connector

RJ45 stands for Registered Jack 45, a type of connector used to connect devices to local area networks (LANs). It is commonly used for Ethernet cables, modems, computers, printers, and more.

History

RJ45 connectors were originally developed for telephone systems in the 1970s. In 1976, the FCC categorized connectors into the Registered Jack (RJ) system to ensure compatibility between consumer equipment and phone company hardware.

Supported Ethernet Standards

RJ45 connectors support a variety of Ethernet standards, including:

  • 10BASE-T
  • 100BASE-TX
  • 1000BASE-T
  • 10GBASE-T
  • 40GBASE-T
